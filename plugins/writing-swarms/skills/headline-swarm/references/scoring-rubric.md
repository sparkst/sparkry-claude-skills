# Headline Scoring Rubric

> **Purpose**: Detailed scoring criteria for 6 headline dimensions
> **Usage**: Reference for headline evaluation and ranking

---

## Overview

Headlines are scored on 6 weighted dimensions (different from soundbites):

| Dimension | Weight | Description |
|-----------|--------|-------------|
| **Curiosity/Hook** | 25% | Does it create intrigue and demand the click? |
| **Clarity** | 20% | Is it instantly understandable? |
| **Promise/Value** | 20% | Is the benefit to the reader clear? |
| **Brevity** | 15% | Word count optimization (ideal: 6-10 words) |
| **Authenticity** | 10% | Does it feel genuine, not clickbait? |
| **SEO Potential** | 10% | Does it contain relevant keywords? |

---

## Dimension 1: Curiosity/Hook (25%)

**Question**: Does it create intrigue and demand the click?

### Scoring Scale

| Score | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| 90-100 | Impossible not to click; creates genuine curiosity gap |
| 70-89 | Strong hook; most readers would click |
| 50-69 | Moderate interest; some would click |
| <50 | Bland; easy to scroll past |

### Indicators (add to base score)

- Open loop (creates question): +15
- Tension/conflict: +20
- Specificity ("your engineers" vs "workers"): +10
- Emotional stakes: +15
- Contrarian angle: +15

### Examples

| Headline | Score | Why |
|----------|-------|-----|
| "What Your Engineers Fear About AI (And Won't Tell You)" | 95 | Open loop + emotional stakes |
| "The Honest AI Conversation Your Team Needs" | 82 | Specificity + promise |
| "AI in the Workplace" | 35 | No hook, generic |

---

## Dimension 2: Clarity (20%)

**Question**: Is it instantly understandable?

### Scoring Scale

| Score | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| 90-100 | Crystal clear on first read |
| 70-89 | Clear with minimal processing |
| 50-69 | Requires re-reading or context |
| <50 | Confusing or ambiguous |

### Indicators

- Simple sentence structure: +15
- Concrete nouns (engineers vs stakeholders): +10
- Active voice: +10
- No jargon: +15
- Single clear idea: +20

### Deductions

- Ambiguous pronouns: -15
- Complex clause structure: -10
- Industry jargon without context: -20
- Multiple competing ideas: -15

### Examples

| Headline | Score | Why |
|----------|-------|-----|
| "Stop Lying to Your Engineers About AI" | 95 | Direct, concrete, single idea |
| "How to Lead Through AI Disruption" | 85 | Clear but slightly generic |
| "Navigating AI Transformation Synergies" | 30 | Jargon-heavy, unclear |

---

## Dimension 3: Promise/Value (20%)

**Question**: Is the benefit to the reader clear?

### Scoring Scale

| Score | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| 90-100 | Immediately clear what reader gets |
| 70-89 | Implied value, reader can infer benefit |
| 50-69 | Vague value proposition |
| <50 | No clear benefit to reader |

### Indicators

- Explicit benefit: +20
- Implied transformation: +15
- How-to promise: +15
- Problem → solution frame: +20
- "For you" specificity: +10

### Examples

| Headline | Score | Why |
|----------|-------|-----|
| "The Script for Your Next AI Conversation" | 95 | Explicit deliverable |
| "How Real Leaders Talk About AI" | 85 | Implied: become a real leader |
| "The Honest Conversation Your Engineers Need" | 82 | Clear but implicit benefit |
| "AI and Your Team" | 40 | No clear benefit |

---

## Dimension 4: Brevity (15%)

**Question**: Is it concise enough for maximum impact?

### Scoring by Word Count

| Word Count | Score |
|------------|-------|
| 5 words | 75 |
| 6 words | 90 |
| 7 words | 95 |
| 8 words | 100 (ideal for headlines) |
| 9 words | 95 |
| 10 words | 90 |
| 11 words | 80 |
| 12 words | 70 |
| 13-15 words | 60 |
| 16+ words | 40 |

### Examples

| Headline | Words | Score |
|----------|-------|-------|
| "The Honest AI Conversation Your Engineers Are Waiting For" | 9 | 95 |
| "Stop Lying to Your Engineers About AI" | 7 | 95 |
| "What Your Engineers Fear About AI (And Won't Tell You)" | 11 | 80 |

---

## Dimension 5: Authenticity (10%)

**Question**: Does it feel genuine, not clickbait?

### Scoring Scale

| Score | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| 90-100 | Feels genuine, earned, true |
| 70-89 | Mostly authentic with minor clickbait elements |
| 50-69 | Somewhat generic or salesy |
| <50 | Pure clickbait, manipulative |

### Red Flags (deduct 20 each)

- "You Won't Believe..."
- "This One Simple Trick..."
- "Everything You Know Is Wrong"
- Number listicles without substance ("37 Ways...")
- ALL CAPS or excessive punctuation!!!
- "Shocking" / "Mind-blowing"
- Hyperbole without substance

### Green Flags (add 10 each)

- Specificity (names, numbers, details)
- Acknowledges complexity
- Honest about limitations
- Direct language without hype
- Voice consistency with content

### Examples

| Headline | Score | Why |
|----------|-------|-----|
| "The Honest AI Conversation Your Team Needs" | 92 | Direct, authentic |
| "What Your Engineers Fear About AI" | 88 | Honest about a real concern |
| "This Mind-Blowing AI Secret Will Transform Your Team!!!" | 25 | Pure clickbait |

---

## Dimension 6: SEO Potential (10%)

**Question**: Does it contain relevant keywords naturally?

### Scoring Scale

| Score | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| 90-100 | Primary keyword front-loaded, natural integration |
| 70-89 | Keywords present, natural placement |
| 50-69 | Minimal keyword presence |
| <50 | No relevant keywords or forced placement |

### Indicators

- Primary keyword in first 3 words: +25
- Secondary keyword present: +15
- Natural integration (not forced): +20
- Search intent alignment: +15

### Note

SEO should never compromise authenticity or clarity. A headline that ranks but doesn't deliver is worse than one that doesn't rank.

### Examples

| Headline | Keywords | Score |
|----------|----------|-------|
| "AI Leadership: The Conversation Your Engineers Need" | AI, Leadership, Engineers | 85 |
| "How to Lead Through AI Disruption" | AI, Lead, Disruption | 80 |
| "The Fear Nobody's Talking About" | None specific | 45 |

---

## Composite Score Formula

```
Overall = (Curiosity * 0.25) +
          (Clarity * 0.20) +
          (Promise * 0.20) +
          (Brevity * 0.15) +
          (Authenticity * 0.10) +
          (SEO * 0.10)
```

---

## Tier Classification

| Tier | Score Range | Action |
|------|-------------|--------|
| **Excellent** | 90-100 | Top candidate, use immediately |
| **Good** | 85-89 | Strong candidate, minor polish |
| **Acceptable** | 75-84 | Passes threshold, consider refinement |
| **Below Threshold** | <75 | Does not advance to cross-ranking |

---

## Minimum Thresholds (Hard Gates)

To advance to cross-ranking, headlines MUST meet:

- **Overall**: >= 75
- **Authenticity**: >= 60 (hard gate - no clickbait)
- **Clarity**: >= 60 (must be understandable)
- **Word Count**: 5-15 words

Failure on ANY hard gate eliminates the candidate.

---

## Tie-Breaking Rules

When candidates have equal aggregated scores:

1. **First**: Higher authenticity score wins
2. **Second**: Higher clarity score wins
3. **Third**: More angles ranked it wins
4. **Fourth**: Shorter word count wins

## Subtitle Scoring

Subtitles complement headlines. Score on:

| Dimension | Weight |
|-----------|--------|
| Complementary (not repetitive) | 30% |
| Specificity/Context | 30% |
| Readability | 20% |
| Length (10-20 words ideal) | 20% |

### Good Subtitle Examples

| Headline | Subtitle | Why Works |
|----------|----------|-----------|
| "The Honest AI Conversation Your Engineers Are Waiting For" | "How to lead through disruption without corporate BS or broken promises" | Adds specificity, complementary |
| "Stop Lying to Your Engineers About AI" | "The truth they need to hear and the framework for delivering it" | Promises deliverable |
