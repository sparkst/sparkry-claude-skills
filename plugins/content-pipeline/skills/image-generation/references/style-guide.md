# Travis Sparks Image Style Guide

> **Last Updated**: 2026-02-22
> **Images Analyzed**: 4 (initial seed)
> **Source**: Manual analysis of top-performing Substack hero images

## Core Aesthetic

**"Real humans empowered by invisible AI"** — Warm, cinematic scenes of authentic people in real-world settings with ephemeral, holographic, or robotic AI elements assisting them. The human is always the protagonist. The AI is the magical helper. Minimal text tells the story. The image does the work.

## Human Subjects

- **Demographics**: Diverse — vary age, gender, ethnicity across images. No single archetype.
- **Clothing**: Casual-authentic — Hawaiian shirts, hoodies, glasses, business casual. Never suits-and-ties corporate.
- **Expressions**: Relaxed confidence, determined focus, playful surprise, genuine emotion. Never fake smiles or stock-photo poses.
- **Actions**: Using phones, working at desks, relaxing on beach, building/tinkering. Always DOING something, never just standing.
- **Representation note**: Include women, people of color, people with glasses, different body types. Avoid "tech bro" homogeneity.

## AI/Tech Elements

- **Visualization**: Holographic, translucent, glowing outlines. Luminous wireframe or soft neon. NOT solid metal robots (except for cartoon/humor pieces).
- **Relationship to humans**: Floating nearby, reaching toward person, organizing floating UI elements, handling tasks. AI serves the human.
- **Opacity**: Semi-transparent, ethereal, glowing edges. The AI should feel magical, not industrial.
- **Color of AI elements**: Electric blue (#0f3460), cyan (#00d4ff), soft purple (#7b2ff7), or warm white glow.
- **Scale**: AI can be human-sized or larger — it represents capability, not a device.
- **Exceptions**: For humor/meme images, cartoon-style robots (simple, blue, friendly) work well in comic-panel format.

## Settings & Environments

- **Top performers**: Beach/tropical (sunset, palm trees, ocean) — conveys freedom + relaxation while AI works
- **Also works**: Home office, kitchen table, workshop/garage, outdoor cafe
- **Lighting**: Golden hour is the default — warm, soft, side-lit. Sunset backgrounds are signature.
- **Atmosphere**: Warm, inviting, aspirational-but-achievable. "You could be here."
- **Avoid**: Cold sterile offices, server rooms, abstract tech backgrounds, dark/dystopian

## Color Palette

- **Primary warmth**: Sunset oranges (#ff6b35), golden yellows (#ffa94d), warm amber
- **AI accent**: Electric blue (#0f3460), cyan glow (#00d4ff), soft purple (#7b2ff7)
- **Text**: Sparkry orange (#ff6b35) for headlines, white (#ffffff) for subtitles
- **Background mood**: Warm gradients (sunset sky, golden hour light)
- **Avoid**: Cold grays, neon greens, harsh reds, all-blue palettes

## Text Overlay

- **Amount**: MINIMAL. 2-7 words maximum. Let the image tell the story.
- **Font style**: Bold, condensed sans-serif. All caps for impact. Think movie poster, not PowerPoint.
- **Placement**: Top 1/3 (preferred) or bottom 1/3. Never center-center (blocks the visual story).
- **Effects**: Slight text shadow or glow for readability. No outlines, no gradients in text.
- **Color**: Sparkry orange (#ff6b35) for primary headline. White for secondary text.
- **Best performing text patterns**:
  - Simple declarative statements: "YOU'RE READY", "30-MINUTE COMPETITIVE RESEARCH"
  - Two-line format: Bold headline + lighter subtitle
  - Text that completes the visual story (image shows situation, text names it)

## Composition

- **Aspect ratio**: 16:9 landscape (default for Substack hero, social sharing)
- **Focal point**: Human subject positioned using rule of thirds (usually right-center or left-center)
- **AI element**: Occupying the opposite third from the human, creating visual tension/balance
- **Negative space**: Leave room at top for text overlay (when used)
- **Depth**: Foreground (person), midground (AI/tech elements), background (environment). Creates cinematic depth.
- **Camera angle**: Slightly low angle (heroic) or eye-level (relatable). Never overhead.

## Style Variants

### Variant A: Cinematic 3D (Primary - highest engagement)
- Pixar/DreamWorks-adjacent 3D rendering
- Slightly stylized proportions (larger eyes, expressive faces)
- Rich textures, volumetric lighting
- Example: "Open Claw" beach image, "You're Ready" workshop image

### Variant B: Photorealistic + AI overlay
- Real-looking photography base
- Holographic/transparent AI elements layered in
- More "editorial" feel
- Example: "30-Minute Competitive Research" image

### Variant C: Comic Panel (for humor/meme content)
- Clean cartoon style, distinct panels
- Simple blue robots as characters
- Speech bubbles, labels, step-by-step
- Example: "A Bot Got Rejected" 3-panel comic

## What Works Best (Performance Data)

- **#1 performer**: "Open Claw" beach image — relaxed man on beach chair, humanoid robot handling chaotic work, sunset tropical setting. **Why**: Perfect contrast between human ease and AI capability. Warm, aspirational. Clear story without reading text.
- Human emotion is key — the more genuine the facial expression, the more engagement
- Beach/outdoor settings consistently outperform office settings
- Images that tell a clear story in one glance beat abstract concepts
- Minimal text with bold fonts beats text-heavy designs
- The juxtaposition of human simplicity + AI complexity is the signature

## What to Avoid

- Stock photo aesthetic (staged, white backgrounds, corporate)
- Uncanny valley faces (too-perfect, waxy skin, dead eyes)
- Abstract technology backgrounds (circuit boards, code rain, binary)
- Dystopian/threatening AI imagery (red eyes, looming robots)
- Too much text (more than 7 words kills engagement)
- All-tech-no-human compositions
- Dark, cold color schemes
- Corporate presentation style
- Clip art or flat icon style (except for comic panel variant)
