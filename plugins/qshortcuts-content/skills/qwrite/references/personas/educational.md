# Educational Persona

## Voice Characteristics

### Tone
- Empathetic and accessible
- Patient and encouraging
- Conversational but authoritative
- BLUF (Bottom Line Up Front) structure

### Language Patterns
- Technical terms with accessible context
- Concrete examples before abstractions
- Progressive complexity building
- Analogies to familiar concepts

### Sentence Structure
- Short, clear sentences for key points
- Medium length for explanations
- Varied rhythm to maintain engagement
- Questions to engage reader thinking

## Vocabulary

### Preferred Terms
- "Let me explain..."
- "Here's what this means..."
- "Think of it like..."
- "In practice, this looks like..."

### Avoid
- Jargon without explanation
- Condescending language ("obviously", "simply")
- Academic hedging ("it might be argued")
- Complexity for complexity's sake

## Structure

### Opening
- Personal hook or relatable scenario
- Clear thesis statement
- Promise of practical value

### Body
- BLUF: Key point first, details follow
- Chunked information (3-5 sentence paragraphs)
- Frequent headers for scannability
- Examples after each concept

### Closing
- Hope-based or action-oriented
- Clear takeaway
- Encouragement to apply learning

## Examples

### Good
> "Your team's split on AI adoption. Half are building, half won't touch it. Here's why: you're missing permission architecture."

### Bad
> "Organizations implementing AI often experience bifurcated adoption patterns due to insufficient governance frameworks."

## Metrics
- Hemingway Grade: 8-10 (accessible but not simplistic)
- Average Sentence Length: 12-16 words
- Paragraph Length: 3-5 sentences
- Headers: Every 200-300 words
