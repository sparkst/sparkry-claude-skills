## Standard Deductions

**IMPORTANT:** OBBBA retroactively bumped 2025 standard deductions. Do NOT use the original Rev. Proc. 2024-40 figures ($15,000 / $30,000 / $22,500) — use the values below.

| Filing Status | 2025 Standard Deduction |
|---|---|
| Single / MFS | **$15,750** |
| MFJ / QSS | **$31,500** |
| Head of Household | **$23,625** |

**Additional standard deduction (per condition — 65+ or blind):**
- Single / HoH (unmarried): $2,000
- MFJ / MFS / QSS (per spouse): $1,600
- Both conditions stack

**NEW — OBBBA Senior Bonus Deduction (2025–2028 only):**
- Additional $6,000 per taxpayer age 65+
- Phaseout: 6% of MAGI over $75,000 single / $150,000 MFJ
- Fully phased out at $175,000 single / $250,000 MFJ
- **Stacks on top of** the existing $1,600/$2,000 age additional
- Expires 12/31/2028

## QBI / Section 199A

**2025 Thresholds:**
| Item | Single | MFJ |
|---|---|---|
| Phaseout begins | $197,300 | **$394,600** |
| Phaseout range | $50,000 | $100,000 |
| Full phaseout (SSTB = $0 deduction) | $247,300 | **$494,600** |

**Specified Service Trades or Businesses (SSTBs)** include: health, law, accounting, actuarial science, performing arts, consulting, athletics, financial services, brokerage services, and any trade where the principal asset is the reputation or skill of employees. Consulting and AI services typically qualify as SSTBs.

Above the full phaseout:
- SSTB: $0 QBI deduction
- Non-SSTB: subject to the W-2 wages / UBIA of qualified property limitation

**OBBBA made §199A permanent.** For 2026, phaseout range widens to $75,000 single / $150,000 MFJ. 2025 figures above are unchanged from pre-OBBBA.

## AMT (Alternative Minimum Tax)

**2025 values (OBBBA did NOT change AMT for 2025 — changes begin 2026):**
| Item | Single | MFJ |
|---|---|---|
| AMT exemption | $88,100 | $137,000 |
| Phaseout begins (AMTI) | $626,350 | $1,252,700 |
| Phaseout fully ends (exemption = $0) | $978,750 | $1,800,700 |

**AMT rate breakpoint:** 26% on AMTI up to $239,100, 28% above.

**AMT preference items to watch for:**
- Specified private activity bond interest (reported on 1099-DIV Box 13)
- ISO exercise bargain element (only if NOT a disqualifying disposition)
- Accelerated depreciation
- Large SALT deductions (now less of an issue post-TCJA since SALT is capped, but still applies for what IS deducted)

**2026 AMT note:** OBBBA tightens AMT phaseout beginning 2026 — thresholds drop to $500k/$1M and phaseout rate doubles from 25% to 50%. More high earners pulled back into AMT in 2026.

## NIIT & Additional Medicare Tax

**Net Investment Income Tax (NIIT) — 3.8%:**
Applies to lesser of net investment income or MAGI over threshold.
| Filing Status | MAGI Threshold |
|---|---|
| Single / HoH | $200,000 |
| MFJ | $250,000 |
| MFS | $125,000 |

**Additional Medicare Tax — 0.9%:**
Applies to wages, compensation, and SE income over threshold.
| Filing Status | Wage Threshold |
|---|---|
| Single / HoH | $200,000 |
| MFJ | $250,000 |
| MFS | $125,000 |

**Neither threshold is indexed for inflation.** Same since 2013.

Employer withholding note: employers withhold the 0.9% on individual wages over $200,000 regardless of filing status. MFJ couples with combined wages over $250k but each under $200k owe the tax but will not have had any withheld — reconcile on Form 8959.

## SALT Cap (OBBBA-modified)

**2025 SALT cap is NOT $10,000 — OBBBA raised it retroactively to 2025.**

| Item | 2025 Value |
|---|---|
| SALT cap (MFJ / Single / HoH) | **$40,000** |
| SALT cap (MFS) | $20,000 |
| Phaseout begins (MAGI) | $500,000 |
| Phaseout rate | 30% of MAGI above threshold |
| Floor (cannot drop below) | **$10,000** |
| MAGI where floor is reached (MFJ) | ~$600,000 |

**Phaseout formula (MFJ):** Cap = max($10,000, $40,000 – 0.30 × (MAGI – $500,000))

**Examples:**
- MAGI $400,000: Cap = $40,000 (no phaseout)
- MAGI $500,000: Cap = $40,000
- MAGI $550,000: Cap = $40,000 – 0.30 × $50,000 = $25,000
- MAGI $600,000: Cap = $40,000 – 0.30 × $100,000 = $10,000
- MAGI $800,000+: Cap = $10,000 (floor)

**Effective:** Tax years 2025–2029 only. Reverts to $10,000 permanently in 2030. Cap and MAGI thresholds increase 1% annually 2026–2029.

**What counts:** State income tax + local income tax + property tax + sales tax (elective). PTET (pass-through entity tax) workarounds remain available — not capped at the entity level.

## Charitable Contributions

**2025 AGI Limits — unchanged from prior law:**

| Contribution Type | AGI Limit |
|---|---|
| Cash to public charity (50% organizations) | 60% |
| Appreciated LTCG property (stock held >1yr) to public charity, FMV basis | **30%** |
| Appreciated property to public charity at cost basis (elective) | 50% |
| Cash to private non-operating foundation | 30% |
| Appreciated property to private foundation | 20% |
| Carryforward for excess | 5 years |

**No AGI floor in 2025.** Full deduction from dollar one of itemized giving.

**2025 is the optimal year for large charitable gifts** — OBBBA adds a 0.5% AGI floor and a 35% benefit cap starting 2026. Donor-advised fund bunching strategy is most valuable before 12/31/2025.

## OBBBA 2025 Provisions

These provisions are **retroactively effective for tax year 2025**. The 2025 return filed in 2026 must reflect them.

1. **Standard deduction bumped** to $15,750 / $23,625 / $31,500
2. **SALT cap raised to $40,000** with phaseout (see above)
3. **Child Tax Credit increased to $2,200** per child ($1,700 refundable). New SSN requirement: both taxpayer AND child must have valid SSNs.
4. **QBI §199A made permanent** (2025 thresholds unchanged from pre-OBBBA; range expands in 2026)
5. **Senior $6,000 deduction** (age 65+, phaseout 6% over $75k/$150k MAGI). Expires 2028.
6. **No Tax on Tips deduction**: up to $25,000 above-the-line. Phaseout over $150k/$300k MAGI. Qualifying occupations only. Expires 2028.
7. **No Tax on Overtime deduction**: up to $12,500 single / $25,000 MFJ (premium portion only — FLSA half of time-and-a-half). Same phaseout. Expires 2028.
8. **100% bonus depreciation** permanently restored for property acquired and placed in service after 1/19/2025. Property placed 1/1/2025–1/19/2025 uses 40% (2025 scheduled rate).
9. **Section 179 cap raised** to $2.5M, phaseout to $4M. 2026 indexed to $2.56M/$4.09M.
10. **Adoption Credit up to $5,000 made refundable** (effective tax years after 12/31/2024).

## OBBBA 2026+ Provisions (NOT yet in effect for 2025)

Do not apply these to 2025 returns. Listed for planning context:

1. **0.5% AGI floor on charitable deductions** (2026+)
2. **35% benefit cap on itemized charitable** (2026+, for top-bracket taxpayers)
3. **Above-the-line charitable for non-itemizers:** $1,000 single / $2,000 MFJ (2026+), excludes DAF and private non-operating foundations
4. **AMT phaseout tightening** (2026+): thresholds drop to $500k/$1M, rate doubles 25% → 50%
5. **QBI phase-in range widens** to $75k/$150k (2026+)
6. **Estate exemption $15M permanent** (decedents 1/1/2026+)
7. **Trump Accounts** (child savings vehicle, funding begins 7/4/2026)
8. **HSA expansion** (telehealth pre-deductible, Bronze/catastrophic eligible, DPC arrangements — 1/1/2026)
