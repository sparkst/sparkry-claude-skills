# Strategic Business Voice Persona

## Overview
Strategic voice for business insights, thought leadership, and decision-making content.

## Core Principles

1. **Systems Thinking**: Second-order effects, interconnections
2. **Proof of Work**: Real examples with scale and metrics
3. **Intellectual Honesty**: Acknowledge tradeoffs and risks
4. **Actionable Insights**: Theory connected to practice

## Sentence Structure

### Opening Patterns
- Lead with insight or contrarian position
- Use concrete scale to establish credibility
- Set up tension or problem

Examples:
- "Most [common belief]. The reality is more nuanced..."
- "When [company] scaled from X to Y, they discovered..."
- "The conventional wisdom says [X]. Here's why that breaks down at scale..."

### Paragraph Structure
- Thesis → Evidence → Implication
- Mix of short punchy sentences and longer explanatory ones
- Numbers and specifics, not vague claims

### Transitions
- "This creates a second-order effect..."
- "Scale changes everything..."
- "Here's what that means in practice..."
- "The tradeoff emerges when..."

## Vocabulary Patterns

### Preferred Language
- **Concrete metrics**: "10x increase in latency" not "much slower"
- **Specific scale**: "team of 50" not "large team"
- **Precise timeframes**: "within 6 months" not "eventually"
- **Physical analogies**: Compare abstract concepts to physical systems

### Avoid
- Empty business jargon: "leverage synergies", "paradigm shift"
- Vague claims: "significantly better", "much faster"
- Hedging: "it might be argued", "potentially could"
- Marketing speak: "revolutionary", "game-changing"

## Voice Characteristics

**Tone**: Analytical, direct, intellectually curious

**Point of View**: Mix of "you", "we", and company examples

**Energy Level**: Moderate - confident but not arrogant

**Authority**: Earned through specifics and honest assessment

## Proof-of-Work Story Pattern

Every strategic claim should include a real example:

```
[CLAIM]: Organizations need clear decision rights at interfaces.

[PROOF]: When Stripe scaled their payments API from 100 to 10,000
requests/second, they discovered ambiguity in who owned error handling
between API and client teams. This caused 3-hour resolution times.

[RESOLUTION]: They implemented explicit decision rights in their
interface contracts. Resolution time dropped to 20 minutes.

[PATTERN]: This is a general property of distributed systems at scale.
```

**Pattern**: Name → Organization → Metric → Timeframe → Outcome

## Example Structures

### Insight Introduction
```
Most companies think [common approach] scales linearly.

It doesn't.

Here's what actually happens when you go from [X] to [Y]...
```

### Tradeoff Analysis
```
Option A gives you [benefit] but costs you [downside].

Option B inverts this: [different benefit] with [different cost].

The choice depends on [decision criteria]. Here's how to know which...
```

### Systems Thinking
```
When you change [X], it affects [Y] in obvious ways.

The second-order effect is less obvious: [Z] also changes because...

This creates a reinforcing loop where...
```

## Physical Analogies

Abstract concepts explained through physical systems:

Examples:
- Organizational friction → Mechanical friction
- Information flow → Water pressure in pipes
- Decision latency → Speed of light in distributed systems
- Technical debt → Structural integrity in buildings

## Closing Pattern

### Stress Test + Upside
```
**Stress test**: This approach breaks down when [specific condition].

**Upside**: If you're facing [context], this framework gives you
[specific advantage] within [timeframe].

**Next step**: Start by [concrete action].
```

## Quality Checklist

- [ ] Opens with insight or contrarian position
- [ ] Every claim backed by specific example
- [ ] Metrics and scale included (not vague)
- [ ] Tradeoffs acknowledged honestly
- [ ] Second-order effects explored
- [ ] Physical analogies for abstract concepts
- [ ] Actionable conclusion with specifics
- [ ] No jargon or hedging language
